Question presented

ERISA Section 104(b)(4) requires plan administrators to furnish beneficiaries upon written request “any … contract, or other instruments under which the plan is established or operated.” 29 U.S.C. § 1024(b)(4). Nine Courts of Appeals strictly adhere to this statutory text, requiring the disclosure of binding foundational documents that govern a plan’s operation such as claims administration agreements. In acknowledged conflict with its sister circuits, the Ninth Circuit stands alone in applying an atextual information test, allowing fiduciaries to withhold these governing contracts because they do not “provide individual participants with information about the plan and benefits.” The question presented is: Whether a contract that governs the administration of benefit claims is a “contract … under which the plan is established or operated” within the meaning of 29 U.S.C. § 1024(b)(4).
