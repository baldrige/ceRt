Question presented

I. Whether the Court’s decision in Franchise Tax Board of California v. Hyatt, 139 S.Ct. 1485 (2019) extends a state’s sovereign immunity to declaratory and/or injunctive relief and to individuals sued in their individual capacity. II. Whether an individual or business that does not have sufficient minimum contacts to be subject to the jurisdiction of a foreign state may seek declaratory or injunctive relief within their home state. III. Whether a state court can employ principles of comity to dismiss an action properly brought pursuant to 42 U.S.C.A. 1983 against a state official in his individual capacity.
