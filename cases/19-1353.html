Questions presented

i QUESTIONS TO BE CONSIDERED There is no uniformity among the Circuit Courts as to the meaning or application of the ERISA statute of limitations for breach of fiduciary duty, 29 U.S.C. §1113.

  1. What gives rise to abandonment of an issue on appeal?

  2. What does the “last action which constitutes [a breach] or a part of the breach” mean in 29 U.S.C. §1113?

  3. Can a litigant rely upon the plain meaning of a statute without abandoning an issue raised in the lower court and raised in their Brief in Chief on appeal to the Circuit Court?

  4. Does the exception to 29 U.S.C. §1113 apply to both the statute of repose, 29 U.S.C. §1113(1), and the statute of limitations provisions 29 U.S.C. §1113(2)?

  5. Does the context in which the word “discovery” as used in the exception contained in 29 U.S.C. §1113 mean actual discovery or constructive discovery?
