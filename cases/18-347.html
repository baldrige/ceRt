Questions presented

  1. Is the plausibility standard attached to federal conspiracy claims pursuant to 42 U.S.C. § 1985 and 42 U.S.C. § 1986 sufficient to protect minorities against private and state-run agencies who discriminate in real-estate transactions in a concerted effort to remove a minority-owned business amidst a mass gentrification of the City post-bankruptcy?

  2. Does Michigan’s Elliott-Larsen Civil Rights Act, Mich. Comp. Laws § 37.2502(1), adequately provide protections to minority business owners in Detroit amidst a mass gentrification of the City?
