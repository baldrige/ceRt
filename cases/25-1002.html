Question presented

A bar association reserves leadership positions for members of specific minority groups. These set-asides violate the State’s antidiscrimination law. But below, the court deemed this otherwise illegal discrimination protected by the First Amendment because the setasides help “express” the bar association’s commitment to “diversity.” The question presented is: Whether the First Amendment overrides antidiscrimination laws when the discrimination furthers the defendant’s views about “diversity,” “equity,” or “inclusion.”
