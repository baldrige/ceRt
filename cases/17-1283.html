Question presented

Medicare Advantage provides Medicare benefits through private insurers known as Medicare Advantage Organizations. Under the relevant regulations, enrollees have a right to “organization determinations” whenever they believe they are entitled to benefits under their Medicare Advantage plans. A multi-step administrative appellate process follows, culminating in judicial review. The Department of Health and Human Services, whose regulations govern the Medicare appeals process, has twice explained in amicus briefs that healthcare providers need not exhaust this Medicare appeals process before suing a Medicare Advantage organization over a refusal to pay the provider when the enrollee no longer has any interest in the outcome. The question presented is as follows: Whether healthcare providers must exhaust the Medicare appeals process before challenging a Medicare Advantage Organization’s refusal to pay for medical care when the enrollee no longer has any interest in the outcome.
