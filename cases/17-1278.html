Question presented

  1. When a Court is confronted with a Motion to Suppress a subpoena issued by a defendant charged with a crime, and the person seeking to quash the subpoena is a third party, that court should not follow the doctrine set out in United States v. Nixon, 418 U.S. 683 (1974) but rather assess the importance of the subpoenaed information to the defendant and the burden imposed on the third party. Unless that burden substantially outweighs the importance of the information sought, it should deny the Motion to Quash.
