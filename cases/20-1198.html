Questions presented

are:

  1. Without evidence of intentional abandonment, can a person abandon their privacy right to an item just by leaving it behind?

  2. Does the doctrine of abandonment from Texas law conflict with the dicta of Riley and present a conflict for this Court’s review?

  3. Does the doctrine of abandonment conflict with other jurisdictions’ consideration of the same issue?

  4. Can a cell phone really be abandoned if its privacy interest is equal to that of a house?
