Question presented

Conﬂicting with Loretto v. Teleprompter Manhattan CATV Corp., 458 U.S. 419 (1982), the Supreme Court of Nevada concluded that to prevail on a physical takings claim a property owner must show that a ﬂood “effectually destroy[ed] or impair[ed] [the property’s] usefulness.” The question presented is: To constitute a taking under the Fifth and Fourteenth Amendments, must a physical invasion also destroy or substantially impair an owner’s economically beneﬁcial uses of property?
