Question presented

Whether the totality of the circumstances test for assessing the reasonableness of a use of force under this Court’s decisions in Tennessee v. Garner, 471 U.S. 1 (1985) and Graham v. Connor, 490 U.S. 386 (1989), means taking into account unreasonable police conduct prior to the use of force that foreseeably created the need to use it, as the First, Third and Tenth Circuits hold or whether the court may only consider the moment of the seizure as the Second and Eleventh Circuits hold and as the divided panel of the Sixth Circuit held in this case.
