Question presented

A party who claims inability to comply with a judgment as a defense to civil contempt must show that the inability was not self-induced. In determining whether such a party has the requisite “clean hands” to invoke the defense, may courts consider prejudgment conduct that contributed to the impossibility of compliance with the judgment?
