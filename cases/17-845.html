Question presented

Whether permanent disbarment of an attorney, with no prior violations, for a violation that did not harm his clients, the court or benefit the attorney in any manner, was such a disproportional punishment that it shocks the conscience of a reasonable person and thus, violate substantive due process. Whether an attorney’s due process right is violated when a court finds him liable, without being charged, or having a fair opportunity to prepare for trial, and whether the court lacked subject matter jurisdiction to rule on other violations.
