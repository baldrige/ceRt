Questions presented

  1. Does this case present an important question not previously decided by the Supreme Court in which an elected member of the Arizona Legislature engaged in exposing corruption by the Executive Branch was ambushed with a sexual harrassment allegation and summarily expelled without due process of law and having no private state constitutional cause of action, based upon a third party report edited illegitimately and deceptively by the proponent of expulsion to remove exculpatory material to the elected representative and containing false statements, instead of having a public Committee hearing involving, inter alia, opening statements, presentation of documents, examination and cross-examination of witnesses and confrontation of accusers?

  2.
