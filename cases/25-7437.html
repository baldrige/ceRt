Question presented

When a district court imposes a 224-month upward variance with no explanation beyond a rote recitation of statutory sentencing factors, and a court of appeals concedes that explanation was clear error, may the court of appeals nonetheless affirm by requiring the defendant to prove his sentence would have been different—a showing made impossible by the very absence of explanation that constitutes the error?
