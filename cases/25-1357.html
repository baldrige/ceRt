Question presented

Major General William T. Cooley was found guilty of one nonconsensual kiss. For that conviction, he was sentenced to forfeit over $50,000 of his pay. The Government demonstrated no harm to any party to justify such a high fine and no state law equivalent permits a fine of this size. Does General Cooley’s sentence to forfeit over $50,000 of his pay constitute an “excessive fine[] imposed” in violation of the Eighth Amendment?
