Question presented

Title 28, section 516, of the United States Code, vests the authority to litigate on behalf of the United States in the Department of Justice, under the supervision of the Attorney General. In turn, section 543 vests the Attorney General with the power to appoint attorneys to assist United States attorneys in the conduct of the litigation. The question presented is whether a district court’s usurpation of the Attorney General’s power to appoint a private attorney to represent the interests of the United States—over the United States’ objection—is subject to harmless error review.
