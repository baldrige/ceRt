Question presented

Did the Ninth Circuit err by its unprecedented holding, in conflict with decisions and the consistent teaching of this Court, that the Fourteenth Amendment and statute 1983 cases permits issuance of an injunction restraining the federal laws of undisputed and legitimate public interest for massive cover up by FBI of use by Russian- Ukrainian KGB agents- terrorists of Russian made high military grade radioactive poisons and toxic elements for assassinations of US citizens on the US territory? Does the Ninth Circuit's application of the "abuse of discretion" standard on appeal in a case involving restriction on Fourteenth Amendment and section 1983 rights merit summary reversal? Does the Ninth Circuit's application of the "abuse of discretion" standard on appeal in a case involving restriction on Fourteenth Amendment and section 1983 cases for violations of US federal laws for use of radioactive weapons, use of weapons of mass destruction, use of chemical weapons on US soil, US patriot act statute for first responds to nuclear terrorism and for refusal to arrest, prosecuted and destroy Russian - Ukrainian nuclear terrorist organization operating on the US territory? Does the Ninth Circuit application of the "abuse of discretion "standard of appeal in a case involving restriction on Fourteenth Amendment and section 1983 cases , were Section 1983 does not provide a specific Statute of Limitation, and when a section 1983 claims accrues is a matter of federal law?
