Question presented

Whether a State statute interpreted by the State’s highest court as vesting in the State’s tax administrative agency the arbitrary and unrestricted discretion to determine how much, if any, monetary interest is owed to a taxpayer on a tax overpayment is unconstitutional because it is in violation of (i) the Takings Clause of the Fifth Amendment made applicable to the States by the Fourteenth Amendment, (ii) the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment, and/or (iii) the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment.
