Question presented

Petitioners are farmers and ranchers in rural North Dakota. On the evening of June 22, 2011, six stray cattle wondered onto Rodney Brossart’s farmstead in Nelson County. Not knowing who the strays belonged to, the Brossarts secured the cows in an abandoned fenced-in missile silo on their land. On the morning of the next day, investigating officials from Nelson County Sheriff’s Department and the North Dakota Stockman’s Association found Rodney and one of his sons on a county road about a mile from his home. They were working on a farm task of pumping water off their cropland across the road and into a ditch. The officers proceeded to ask Rodney questions about the stray cattle, and Rodney told them that he was busy at the moment. The investigation escalated and within a couple of minutes, Deputy Sheriff Eric Braathen used his Taser on Rodney, and he rolled into the wet, muddy ditch. Then, Deputy Braathen followed Rodney into the ditch and tased him seven more times within five minutes. Rodney was arrested and placed in the squad car where he passed out. An ambulance was called. The next day, Rodney’s son, Thomas, was arrested on the farmstead, held face down at gun point, handcuffed behind his back, and placed in the back seat of the squad car. Then, Deputy Braathen tased him without warning on his leg because he did not move over.
