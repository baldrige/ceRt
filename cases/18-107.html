Supreme Court of the United States · No. 18-107

R.G. & G.R. Harris Funeral Homes, Inc. v. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, et al.

Paid petition · United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit, No. 16-2424 · judgment March 7, 2018

Decided · June 15, 2020
Pre-decision estimate: 3% cert probability

Questions presented

  1. Whether the word "sex" in Title VII's prohibition on discrimination "because of ... sex," 42 U.S.C. 2000e-2(a)(l), meant "gender identity" and included "transgender status" when Congress enacted Title VII in 1964.

  2. Whether Price Waterhouse v. Hopkins, 490 U.S. 228 (1989), prohibits employers from applying sex-specific policies according to their employees' sex rather than their gender identity. GRANTED LIMITED TO THE FOLLOWING QUESTION: WHETHER TITLE VII PROHIBITS DISCRIMINATION AGAINST TRANSGENDER PEOPLE BASED ON

  3. THEIR STATUS AS TRANSGENDER OR (2) SEX STEREOTYPING UNDER PRICE WATERHOUSE v. HOPKINS, 490 U. S. 228 (1989).
