Question presented

Could a jurist of reason construe 28 USC § 2244(d)(1)(C) to include decisions by a state Supreme Court as well as the United States Supreme Court? Did the trial court denied petitioner his Fourteenth Amendment Due Process right to a fair trial, and Sixth Amendment right to proof beyond a reasonable doubt before a jury, by instructing the jury that they could find petitioner guilty of premeditated and deliberated first degree murder as the natural and probable consequence of the offense of discharging a firearm from a vehicle, given the California Supreme Court’s later construction of the murder statute that forbids finding an accomplice guilty of that type of murder on a natural and probable consequences theory of liability?
