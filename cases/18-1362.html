Question presented

This Court has long recognized an exception to the mootness doctrine for a controversy that is “capable of repetition, yet evading review.” S. Pac. Terminal Co. v. ICC, 219 U.S. 498, 515 (1911). The courts of appeals are split over how this exception applies to cases involving elections. This case presents the following question: Under what circumstances can a candidate continue to challenge a ballot-access rule after the election over which he originally sued has passed?
