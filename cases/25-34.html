Question presented

The Fourth Circuit refused to compel arbitration because it applied a rule of Maryland law that requires arbitration provisions, unlike any other contractual term, to contain their own unique exchange of consideration. The question presented is: Does the Federal Arbitration Act, 9 U.S.C. § 2, permit courts to apply a heightened consideration standard solely to contractual arbitration provisions?
