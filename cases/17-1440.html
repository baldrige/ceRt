Questions presented

American Pipe & Constr. Co. v. Utah, 414 U.S. 538, 94 S. Ct. 756, 38 L.Ed.2d 713 (1974) and Crown, Cork & Seal Co. v. Parker, 462 U.S. 345, 350, 103 S. Ct. 2392, 76 L. Ed. 2d 628 (1983) hold that the commencement of a class-action lawsuit tolls the statute of limitations for absent class members until the existence and limits of the class have been established. The First, Third, Fifth, Sixth and Eleventh Circuits interpret that to mean that tolling continues throughout the litigation of the classcomplaint including appeals. The Seventh Circuit holds that tolling stops immediately when a district court dismisses a class-action complaint with prejudice before a class certification determination is made and does not continue while the dismissal is appealed.

  1. Whether American Pipe tolling ceases immediately when a class-action lawsuit is dismissed with prejudice before a determination on class certification is made by the district court.

  2. Whether the answer to question number 1 is different if the dismissal was erroneous and reversed on appeal.
