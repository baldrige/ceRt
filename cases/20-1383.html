Questions presented

  1. Is there a good-faith defense to 42 U.S.C. § 1983 that shields a defendant from damages liability for depriving citizens of their constitutional rights if the defendant acted under color of a law before it was held unconstitutional?

  2. Are employees who had compulsory union fees seized from them in violation of their First Amendment rights prior to Janus v. AFSCME, Council 31, 138 S. Ct. 2448 (2018), entitled to damages or restitution for their injuries?
