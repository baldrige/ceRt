Questions presented

  1. Whether the Fourteenth Amendment prohibits a State from mandating that an individual accept the administration of a drug that is exempt from 21 U.S.C. § 355(a) — on penalty of losing benefits — where the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act expressly requires the Secretary to ensure such administration is the product of voluntary, informed consent.

  2. Whether a State mandate for an individual to accept the administration of a covered countermeasure is consistent with the Fourteenth Amendment insofar as the PREP Act’s immunity clause of 42 U.S.C. § 247d6d(a)(1), upon administration, extinguishes the right to access courts if injured by the countermeasure.
