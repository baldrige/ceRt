Question presented

1a. This Court has permitted “limited discovery” in cases where the defendant has asserted the affirmative defense of qualified immunity in its answer. To what extent should discovery be allowed from third-party eyewitnesses when the only evidence in support of “what actually happened” is a soundless video and the defendants’ self-serving testimony? 1b. This Court has held that if video evidence “blatantly contradicts” the plaintiff’s testimony, a trial court may conclude that there is no genuine issue of material fact in dispute that would preclude summary judgment. But circuit courts of appeals throughout the country have held that soundless video does not provide conclusive evidence that would permit judgment as a matter of law. Did the lower courts err by denying Petitioner’s request for limited discovery when the only evidence in support of Respondents’ qualifiedimmunity defense was a soundless video and their self-serving testimony? 2.
