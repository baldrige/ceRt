Question presented

I. Whether Missouri’s medical marijuana law (Mo. Const. art. XIV) is preempted by the federal statute. II. If not, since there is no verdict director for the statutory criteria under section 195.017.1, whether the Due Process Clauses, by and through Marbury v. Madison, 5 U.S.137 (1803), may be extended beyond rational basis review. III. If so, whether Missouri’s medical marijuana law or the medical marijuana law of any state where it was legislatively enacted means marijuana, because of the word “no” in the statutory criteria, no longer satisfies the statutory criteria and therefore section 195.017.2(4)(w) is unconstitutional.
