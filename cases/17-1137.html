Question presented

On June 12, 2017, the Court granted review in Oil States Energy Services, LLC v. Greene’s Energy Group, LLC, No. 16-712, 2017 U.S. LEXIS 3727, at *1 to consider the following question presented: Whether inter partes review—an adversarial process used by the Patent and Trademark Office (PTO) to analyze the validity of existing patents—violates the Constitution by extinguishing private property rights through a non-Article III forum without a jury? Id. This case also involves an inter partes review proceeding in which the PTO invalidated Petitioner’s patent and, therefore, this petition presents the same question as Oil States.
