Question presented

I. RELIEF REQUESTED This case involves a private citizen (plaintiff) v public servants (defendants) who are legally contracted to each other solely under Washington State's constitution and laws mandating defendants exercise their "just powers to protect and maintain individual (plaintiff's) rights." [Article 1 ,section 1]. Therefore this Court should, Compel the 9th Circuit to, or show cause why it does not, apply Washington State's laws that implement Article 1, section 1 as the US 10th amendment provides, 28 USC 1652 demands, and 28 USC 2072(b) prohibits "abridging, modifying or enlarging". Compel the 9th Circuit to exercise its fiduciary obligations to hold its 'officers of the court' to their legal and ethical duty required by Washington's laws as rules FRAP 46, circuit rule 46-2 and LCR 83.3 require. Or, provide plaintiff a forum, which is impartial as 28 USC 455(a) and (b)(4) mandates, to address judges-judging-judges who abridge, modify, or enlarge statutory rights in violation of §2072(b).
