In those 106 years not one copyright trial was limited or controlled by the deposit, which was usually an incomplete outline of the song. The Ninth Circuit’s en banc decision, holding that only paper sheet music deposits have copyright protection under the 1909 Act, will likely divest hundreds of thousands of songs of copyright protection. Thus, a question of first impression arises for this Court under the Copyright Act of 1909: Whether or not the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals sitting en banc correctly held that musical copyright protection under the Copyright Act of 1909 is limited to sheet music deposits—and only sheet music deposits—or whether songs under the 1909 Act are protected as created and fixed in a tangible medium?
