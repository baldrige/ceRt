Question presented

Whether the unit of prosecution under 18 U.S.C. § 201(b) is defined by each discrete "official act" performed in exchange for a thing of value, or whether multiple official acts undertaken pursuant to an ongoing stream of substantial financial benefits may be collapsed into a single continuing bribery offense based on a unified quid pro quo. Whether the judicial consolidation of multiple, distinct official acts into a single count improperly converts a "count-by-act" statute into a "course-of-conduct" offense, in a manner contrary to the plain text and structural design of 18 U.S.C. § 201(b).
