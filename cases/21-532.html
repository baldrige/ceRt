Question presented

Can three or four unadorned “calls for service” to an area (1) constitute a specific and articulable fact significantly associating said area with criminal activity, (2) support an officer’s reasonable suspicion of those found therein, and (3) justify a warrantless search and seizure despite the plain text of the Fourth Amendment and “no indication that the person was engaged in criminal activity other than sitting there in the dark”?
