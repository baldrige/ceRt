Question presented

QUESTION ONE: Whether, in light of a profound and acknowledged circuit split, a district court may deny a § 2255 hearing by making a dispositive credibility determination against a petitioner based on an ambiguous and contested paper record, or whether § 2255(b) and this Court’s precedent in Machibroda v. United States, 368 U.S. 487 (1962), require a Eve evidentiary hearing to resolve such material factual disputes? Cooper, 566 U.S. 156 (2012), thereby failing to conduct the deliberate, petitioner-favorable threshold inquiry mandated by this Court’s decisions in Miller-El v.
