Questions presented

  1. Whether a confirmed arbitration award on related or overlapping claims may be given issue-preclusive effect so as to bar a Sarbanes-Oxley (“SOX”) whistleblower claim, notwithstanding 18 U.S.C. § 1514A(e)’s explicit prohibition against forcing SOX claims into predispute arbitration.

  2. Whether the court of appeals’ holding that an arbitration award confirmed by a district court triggers full preclusive effect over a statutorily nonarbitrable SOX claim contravenes the narrow rule set forth in Alexander v. Gardner-Denver Co., 415 U.S. 36

  3. and reaffirmed in 14 Penn Plaza LLC v. Pyett, 556 U.S. 247 (2009), which bars arbitral preclusion of statutory claims that were never authorized for arbitration in the first place.
