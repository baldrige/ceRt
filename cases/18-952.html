Question presented

Whether the rule of United States v. Gaudin, 515 U.S. 506 (1995)—namely, that the Sixth Amendment requires juries to find not just historical facts but also that those facts satisfy the legal definitions of elements of offenses—applies to “sentencing factors” that are covered by Apprendi v. New Jersey, 530 U.S. 466 (2000), and its progeny.
