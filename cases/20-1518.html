Question presented

As this Court has recognized, the correct delineation of the dimensions of a bankruptcy “proceeding” is a matter of considerable importance. Ritzen Group, Inc. v. Jackson Masonry, LLC, 140 S. Ct. 582, 587 (2020). Erroneous identification either way – of an interlocutory order as a final decision, or of a final order as interlocutory – has significant detrimental impacts upon litigation. Id. The question presented is: Whether a Bankruptcy Appellate Panel’s order reversing a bankruptcy court’s dismissal of an involuntary petition constitutes a final, appealable order.
