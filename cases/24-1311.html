Question presented

Kimble v. Marvel Enterprises, LLC, 576 U.S. 446 (2015) upheld the rule announced in Brulotte v. Thys Co., 379 U.S. 29 (1964) that an agreement to pay royalties on a patent after its expiration is patent misuse and unlawful per se. The Kimble majority did not defend the reasoning of Brulotte, relying instead on stare decisis. Three justices would have overruled “our obvious mistake” in Brulotte. 576 U.S. at 472 (Alito, J. dissenting). There is a worsening split among the circuits on how to apply the Brulotte rule.
