Supreme Court of the United States · No. 25-5964

Oladayo Oladokun v. United States

IFP petition · United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit, No. 23-6141 · judgment January 24, 2025

Certiorari denied · March 2, 2026

Questions presented

  1. Whether the Sentencing Guidelines’ commentary promulgated without notice-and-comment rulemaking retains binding authority after Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo,603 U.S. 369 (2024), which repudiated deference to agencies' statutory interpretations, and Kisor v. Wilkie, 588 U.S. 558 (2019), which narrowed deference to agencies' regulatory interpretations.

  2. Whether the definition of "loss" in the commentary to the U.S.S.G. § 2B1.1(b)(1), which expands the term to include "intended loss," exceeds the Sentencing Commission's authority under 28 U.S.C. § 994(a) and violates the seperation of powers by effectively amending the guidelines without congressional; or judical approval.
