Question presented

I. If, after the seating of a jury in a criminal trial, the judge conducting the trial engages in improper contact with a juror, must an evidentiary hearing be conducted pursuant to Remmer v. United States, 347 U.S. 227 (1954) to determine whether such contact constituted harmless error? II. Is it the burden of defense counsel to request an evidentiary hearing pursuant to Remmer v. United States, 347 U.S. 227 (1954) upon his learning of a judge’s improper contact with a juror outside of the courtroom or is the hearing mandated to be held regardless of whether a request is made?
