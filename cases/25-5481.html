Questions presented

  1. Federal law bans the possession of firearms by anyone who has ever been convicted of a crime punishable by more than one year of imprisonment. 18 U.S.C. § 922(g)(1). How should courts decide whether an individual prosecution or conviction under that statute is consistent with the Second Amendment to the Constitution? 2. Under the prevailing interpretation of the nexus-with-commerce element of the federal possession ban, a former felon possesses “in or affecting commerce” a firearm if the firearm was made in another state. Does Congress have the constitutional authority to enact such a law? DIRECTLY RELATED PROCEEDINGS United States v. Olivas, No. 2:23-cr-64 (N.D. Tex.) United States v. Olivas, No. 24-10268 (5th Cir.)
