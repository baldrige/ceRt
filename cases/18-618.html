Question presented

Is the Federal Arbitration Act’s goal of “providing efficient streamlined procedures tailored to the type of dispute,” AT&T Mobility, LLC v. Concepcion, 563 U.S. 333, 334-35 (2011), compromised by allowing a defendant to forego arbitration against named class representative plaintiffs, and then successfully assert arbitration years later against the subsequently certified class members?
