Questions presented

This case concerns whether the Federally Recognized Indian Tribe List Act (“List Act”) of 1994, still has judiciary branch tribal recognition or whether it could be removed from the “List Act” administratively without a rule or guideline. The questions presented are:

  1. Whether the way to effectuate judiciary branch tribal recognition is through Writ of Mandamus or Declaratory and Injunctive Relief, because the agency admitted it does not have administrative procedures for judiciary branch tribal recognition.

  2. Whether the Supreme Court accepts because the previous rulings in Kanam v. Haaland, No. 22- 5197, 2023 WL 3063526, at *1 (D.C. Cir. Apr. 25, 2023), were void for lack of jurisdiction.
