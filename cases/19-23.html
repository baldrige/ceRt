Question presented

Does requiring a wrongly-named Responsible Operator to issue benefits to a Claimant with normal pulmonary function and normal arterial blood gas testing under the Black Lung Benefits Act violate the Administrative Procedure Act when such result stems from amended agency regulations that (1) provide different time limits to submit evidence for opposing parties, (2) shift burdens of proof, and (3) create heightened rebuttal burdens that cannot be met, or is the Black Lung Benefits Act exempt from Administrative Procedure Act governance as claimed by the Secretary of Labor?
