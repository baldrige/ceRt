Supreme Court of the United States · No. 25-5133

Alexander P. Bebris v. National Center for Missing and Exploited Children

IFP petition · United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit, No. 24-5067 · judgment January 6, 2025

Certiorari denied · October 6, 2025

Questions presented

  1. Does an entity, regardless of its method or location of its incorporation, bedome an "authority" of the United States when;

  2. the majority of funding for said entity is provided by the federal government; (2) that entity is specifically named and directed by statute to perform particular functions and actions; f:' and, (3) a subset of the directed statutory mandates include the exercise of power(s) [law enforcement] that have been reserved to the sovereign?

  3. If an entity is found to be an "authority" of the United States in meeting question #1 in the affirmative, is that entity subject to the provisions of 5 USC 552 ("The Freedom of Information Act")?

  4. Does an entity, regardless of how and where it is incorporated, become a "de facto" agency of the United States under Article II of the United States Constitution where that entity has been granted a special law enforcement authority by Congress? i.
