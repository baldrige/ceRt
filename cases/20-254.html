Questions presented

On May 2, 2017, the U.S. attorney’s office for northern California instituted a criminal action against petitioner. On December 19, 2017, the U.S. district court for northern California committed petitioner to competence restoration at Federal Medical Center, Butner, North Carolina. As a result, petitioner applied for a writ of habeas corpus with the U.S. district court for eastern North Carolina on April 5, 2018. The latter district court dismissed the application on September 17,

  1. The denial led to petitioner’s September 28, 2018 complaint based on Federal Tort Claims Act (“FTCA”). The FTCA complaint challenges the constitutionality of certain federal statutes as well. On October 30, 2018, the California district court dismissed the criminal case without prejudice. On October 2, 2019, the district court for eastern North Carolina dismissed the FTCA case. U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit upheld said dismissal on March 12, 2020 and denied the ensuing petition for rehearing en banc on May 26,

  2. Thus, the following questions are presented for review:

  3. Should a three-judge court instead of a single judge at the North Carolina district court adjudge the FTCA case?

  4. Does denial of certiorari justify dismissal of said FTCA case? (i)
