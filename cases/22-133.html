Questions presented

  1. Whether dismissing an appeal when a fee waiver Motion is pending is constitutional.

  2. Whether dismissing the appeal after annihilating the record and falsely claiming the Appeal was not prosecuted against evidence1 to the contrary, is constitutional.

  3. Whether not considering material intrinsic evidence of Patent Prosecution History2 makes it an improperly decided case.

  4. Whether an improperly decided case3 falsely claimed as precedent and basis for collateral estoppel is constitutional.

  5. Whether Trustees of Dartmouth College v. Woodward, 17 U.S. 518 (1819) was properly decided against impairing the obligation of contract.
