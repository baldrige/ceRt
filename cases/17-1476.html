Questions presented

Police served a felony narcotics warrant. Officer W.F. Snow encountered the primary suspect, Jermaine Darden, a 340-pound man, apparently robust, but suffering from extraordinary latent health infirmities, who had ingested a toxic synthetic cannabinoid. As seen on video, Darden struggled against efforts to secure him. Officer J. Romero eventually helped. Darden then suffered a fatal heart attack (a death the medical examiner ruled to have been from natural causes).

  1. Did the Fifth Circuit err in reversing the district court’s grant of qualified immunity to the officers by basing its analysis only on Darden’s subjective reasons for resisting?

  2. Did the Fifth Circuit err by failing to identify any appropriate clearly established law, given it effectively declared a fact issue to exist only as to why (not if) Darden struggled against the officers, and then defined clearly established law only by the high-level and inapplicable proposition that officers cannot use force against a non-resisting suspect?
