Question presented

Whether lab results requested not by law enforcement but by a forensic pathologist to assist in making a routine cause of death determination are testimonial in nature and their admission without cross-examination of the analyst violates a criminal defendant’s right to confrontation as articulated in Crawford v. Washington, 541 U.S. 36, 51 (2004), Melendez–Diaz v. Massachusetts, 557 U.S. 305 (2009), and Ohio v. Clark, 576 U.S. 237 (2015).
