Question presented

Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 60(b)(4) requires courts to vacate judgments entered outside the issues pleaded. Reynolds v. Stockton, 140 U.S. 254 (1891). The question here is whether the law-of-the-case doctrine permits a court to skip that determination. The question presented is: Whether Rule 60(b)(4) requires a court to vacate a judgment that decides a matter entirely outside the issues pled, and prohibits a court from using the law of the case doctrine to avoid making that determination.
