Questions presented

  1. Whether jury hesitation to impose the death penalty, such as in this case where five jurors initially voted for life despite the jury not knowing any of the compelling mitigation evidence trial counsel failed to investigate or present, must be considered in the evaluation of the prejudice prong of the ineffective assistance of counsel test set forth in Strickland v. Washington, 466 U. S. 668 (1984), as some circuits have held in conflict with the decision of the Eleventh Circuit.

  2. Whether the Eleventh Circuit’s determination that petitioner’s trial counsel’s deficient performance did not prejudice him conflicts with Rompilla v. Beard, 545 U. S. 374 (2005), and Williams v. Taylor, 529 U. S. 362 (2000), where Rompilla’s facts are materially indistinguishable from those herein presented.
