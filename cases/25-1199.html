Questions presented

  1. Whether, as this Court suggested in Slack v. McDaniel, 529 U.S. 473, 484 (2000), the existence of a circuit split alone, notwithstanding contrary home circuit precedent, can demonstrate that reasonable jurists could find a claim debatable for purposes of granting a certificate of appealability under 28 U.S.C. § 2253(c)(2). (A 3-3 circuit split.)

  2. Whether collateral challenges to noncustodial punishment, such as forfeiture orders or restitution awards, can be cognizable under § 2255 if the financial penalty is sufficiently severe that it restrains liberty. (A 5-2 circuit split.)
