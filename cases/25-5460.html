Question presented

In Beard v. United States, 158 US. 550 (1895) and Brown v. United States, 256 US. 335 (1921), this Court recognized the common law right to stand one’s ground and to prevent the commission of a violent felony. In a prosecution for homicide in violation of 18 U.S.C. § 1111 or § 1112, does the right to self-defense include the common law right to stand one’s ground and to defend against a violent or forcible felony?
