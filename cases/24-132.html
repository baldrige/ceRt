Question presented

Whether a Court fails to follow the law, is a legal question. Whether a Court’s holding expands jurisdiction is a matter of law. Where the only issue presented by a party throughout the entire case (until the appeal) is the same disputed fact, is an evidence sufficiency claim recast as a legal argument, and it should fail as a matter of law. See McGraw v. Madison Twp, 231 F. App'x 419; 422 (CA 6, 2007) (The defendants' efforts to recast their evidence sufficiency claims as a legal argument are without merit.
