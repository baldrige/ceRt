Question presented

QUESTIONS PRESNTED Petitioner, a former U.S. Army military police sergeant; retired NYC police sergeant with 350 career arrests and a member of the bar of this Court since May 31 , 1988 effected a citizen's arrest (videotaped) of an off-duty police sergeant who had damaged (felony) a window of petitioner's car. The sergeant in turn arrested petitioner. Petitioner was charged with menacing, i.e., displaying a sheathed hatchet; criminal possession of a weapon (the sheathed hatchet) and endangering the sergeant's daughter. Petitioner unsuccessfully moved to dismiss on 211d Amendment and other grounds. At trial petitioner also unsuccessfully moved to dismiss on 2nd Amendment and other grounds. He was denied justification and 2°d Amendment jury charges and prohibited from cross-examining the sergeant about a prior bad act because the se1·geant had been gi·anted immunity in a previous federal civil case (exception gi·anted on the record). Petitioner was convicted of the menacing charge and acquitted of the weapons possession and endangering charges, a clearly repugnant verdict, but the judge dismissed the jury anyway. ignoring his statutory duty to send it back. Petit.ioner appealed to no avail. The appellate court held his issues were not preserved or were ·without merit even though petitioner made a written motion during trial and was gi·anted the exception. The questions presented are: (1) Does the 2nd Amendment apply here; (2) Does the granting of immunity in a federal civil case prohibit cross examination of a complaining witness about the admitted underlying bad act; (3) was deliberately failing to send the jury back to deliberate a violation of due process: (4) Was the appellate court's failure to rule a violation of due process?
