Question presented

Whether the United States Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit (“Eighth Circuit”) improperly applied Chevron deference1 to a Federal Communications Commission (“FCC”) order under the Pole Attachments Act concluding that 47 U.S.C. § 224(d) and § 224(e), which unambiguously set forth two separate and different attachment rate formulas, can provide for identical rates where the FCC accorded the word “cost” in § 224(e) an infinite number of meanings unrelated to the surrounding statutory language? 1 See Chevron U.S.A., Inc. v. Natural Resources Defense Council, Inc., 467 U.S. 837 (1984).
