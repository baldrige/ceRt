Question presented

A decision of the 11th circuit's issued in 2009 for United Tech. Corp. v. Mazer, concluded that, once a defendant challenges personal jurisdiction, the burden "shifts back to the plaintiff to produce evidence to support jurisdiction" that the plaintiff , failed to establish through its allegations or competent evidence that [defendant] [was] subject to personal jurisdiction, and that due process was not reached." In its opinion below, the Eleventh Circuit affirmed a decision in regards to personal jurisdiction that contradicts with its prior opinion and other circuits decisions.
