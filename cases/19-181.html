Questions presented

Federal law requires Medicaid recipients to pay from their income received a portion of their monthly nursing home bill, called the resident’s “liability.” Two important questions of federal law arise regarding how to calculate a resident’s liability:

  1. Did the Indiana Court of Appeals incorrectly determine a question of federal law by ruling that, when calculating the resident’s liability, a state Medicaid agency must include garnished income in “income received,” thus creating a conflict with the South Dakota Supreme Court?

  2. In determining this question of federal law, was it error for the Indiana Court of Appeals to defer to the state Medicaid agency’s interpretation of “income received” in 42 C.F.R. § 435.725?
