Question presented

Does the Rooker-Feldman Doctrine bar the District Court's review of Petitioner's Complaint to determine if divorced or separated Parents with joint custody of their child who provide a home, medical insurance, food, clothing and access to education for their child and do not receive Government assistance such as ADFC or TANF, are to be considered "absent", "non-custodial" Parents for the purposes of child support payments under Section of Title IV of the Social Security Act? (42 U.S.C. §651-670) Does the Rooker-Feldman Doctrine bar the District Court's review of Petitioner's Complaint due to injuries that were independent from the state-court judgments made by Defendants who were nonparties to the state-court judgements; to determine if these state-court judgments were procured by fraud, unjust enrichment, misrepresentations and other improper means, and for declaratory and injunctive relief from the injuries brought on by the threat of future unconstitutional enforcement actions of Federal regulations, State laws and customs, and when Petitioners are denied their civil rights and equal rights in the State of California Courts and to remove these proceedings to the District Court under 28 U.S.C. § 1443, and as a class action under 28 U.S.C. § 1453?
