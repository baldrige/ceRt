Question presented

A shareholder seeking to bring a derivative claim on behalf of a corporation ordinarily must plead with particularity, as a precondition to prosecuting the representative action, that making a demand on the corporation’s board to pursue the claim would be futile. The question presented is: If a court determines that a complaint filed by one shareholder does not plead demand futility with the requisite particularity, does the Due Process Clause and this Court’s decisions in Taylor v. Sturgell, 553 U.S. 880 (2008), and Smith v. Bayer Corp., 564 U.S. 299 (2011), permit binding other shareholders that were not parties to the initial litigation to that decision, thus barring consideration of whether a new complaint filed by those shareholders pleads sufficient allegations to demonstrate that pre-suit demand was futile?
