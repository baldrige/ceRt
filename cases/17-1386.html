Questions presented

  1. Whether the Tenth Circuit had jurisdiction to hear Deputy Wilson’s appeal.

  2. Whether upon exercising jurisdiction, the Tenth Circuit should have determined that the District Court erred in denying qualified immunity to Deputy Wilson by disregarding ALL of a law enforcement officer’s testimony, including those parts that are not contradicted by any evidence in the record, in order to create a dispute in fact.

  3. Whether upon exercising jurisdiction, the existing clearly established law would make it clear to a reasonable law enforcement officer that he would violate the law by using deadly force against an individual who turned and pointed a gun at him in a hostile motion.
