Question presented

In a provision of the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976, Congress delegated to the United States Department of the Interior authority to withdraw, for up to 20 years, large tracts of federal lands from availability for mineral development under the Mining Law of 1872, as amended. Congress included in the same provision a right of legislative veto over any large withdrawal, but all now agree the legislative veto violates the Presentment Clause. The question presented is: Can Congress’s delegation to the Department of the Interior of withdrawal authority over large tracts of land survive without the legislative veto right that Congress included as a check on the exercise of that authority?
