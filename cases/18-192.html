Question presented

Federal law contains a number of crimes for which the only statutorily authorized punishments are death or life imprisonment without possibility of parole. As the Government conceded below, this Court has held that the Constitution forbids imposing either punishment on a person who was under the age of eighteen at the time of the crime. The question presented is: Whether the Due Process Clause forbids the Government from prosecuting an individual who was a juvenile at the time of the crime under a statute that provides no punishment that can constitutionally be applied to that individual.
