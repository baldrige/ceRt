Question presented

Question 1: Whether Jacobson v. Massachusetts, 197 U.S. 11 (1905), forecloses a substantive due process claim under the Fourteenth Amendment that an individual has a right to refuse unwanted investigational drugs without incurring a penalty or losing a public benefit to which the individual is otherwise entitled. Question 2: Whether the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act and the Public Readiness and Emergency Preparedness Act preempt states from conditioning employment in a state-licensed healthcare facility on an individual’s receipt of investigational drugs.
