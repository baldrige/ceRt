Questions presented

In Gertz v. Robert Welch Inc • t 418 US 323(1974), Justice Powell, in reference to Defamation/Libel claims of a private individual concerning an issue of public concern, stated: "under the 1st Amendment there is no such thing as a false idea... (it) requires that we protect some falsehood in order to protect speech that matters". Applying that principle, a majority of lower courts to consider the issue have recognized that the privileges granted by the First and Fourteenth Amendments; and Laws enacted by Congress, immunizes citizens from Defamation liability and requires the standard of proof to be that of "Actual Malice". The Massachusetts Courts have split from that consensus causing a crucial need to seek the guidance of the Supreme Court. Accordingly, the questions presented are:
