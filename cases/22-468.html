Question presented

Following the Court’s ruling in Timbs v. Indiana, 139 S. Ct. 682 (2019), which held that the Excessive Fines Clause of the Eighth Amendment is incorporated against the states via the Fourteenth Amendment, does a state court err by failing to apply any of the factors set out in United States v. Bajakajian, 524 U.S. 321 (1998) in evaluating whether a civil penalty is unconstitutionally excessive?
