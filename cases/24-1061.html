Question presented

Oregon’s audio recording law—a national outlier— requires “specifically inform[ing]” anyone in almost any conversation that their words are being recorded. This peculiar requirement severely hampers modern investigative journalism, under mining the First Amendment’s protection of free speech and newsgathering by effectively prohibiting the use of today’s most powerful reporting tools—discreet audio recordings.
