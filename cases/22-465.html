Question presented

CERCLA’s three-year statute of limitations for an “action for contribution for any response costs or damages” under § 113(f) begins running on “the date of judgment in any action under [CERCLA] for recovery of such costs or damages.” 42 U.S.C. § 9613(g)(3)(A). Does a bare declaratory judgment that determines liability but imposes no “costs” and awards no “damages” trigger that statute of limitations?
