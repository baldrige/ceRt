Question presented

A prosecutor had Petitioner’s jail telephone calls recorded, listened to them, made notes from them and had at least one witness listen to them. This blatant violation of Petitioner’s Sixth Amendment rights should have resulted in dismissal of the charge under United States v. Morrison, 449 U.S. 361, 365-366 (1981). The district court held otherwise and the Fifth Circuit denied a certificate of appealability. Did this denial constitute reversible error?
