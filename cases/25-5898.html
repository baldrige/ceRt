Supreme Court of the United States · No. 25-5898

Jeremiah Bobb v. United States

IFP petition · United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit, No. 23-3748 · judgment April 1, 2025

Certiorari denied · November 24, 2025

Question presented

A child-witness’s initial interview with the authorities was recorded and the child un-equivocally stated that the Petitioner did not sexually assault/molest her in any way. Due to a failure to understand and apply the rules of evidence, counsel failed to place the exculpatory statement into evidence. The question presented is: Where evidentiary legal error by a court combined with ineffective assistance of counsel led to the failure to place into trial evidence a prior denial by a child that the defendant had ever sexually assaulted her, should a so-called "strategic decision" be allowed to excuse the violation of the Sixth Amendment right to effective assistance of counsel?
