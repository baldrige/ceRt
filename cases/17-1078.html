Questions presented

  1. Under 42 U.S.C. § 1983, is a public official, whose reckless conduct proximately causes another official to violate a plaintiff ’s federally protected right, liable for the plaintiff ’s injuries, even though the latter official is entitled to qualified immunity?

  2. When a public official violates clearly established law through his pre-seizure conduct, and the conduct causes the need to use deadly force, is the official protected by qualified immunity?
