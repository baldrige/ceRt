Question presented

  1. Whether the “misrepresentation exception,” 28 U.S.C. § 2680(h), of the Federal Tort Claims Act bars claims for aiding and abetting a breach of fiduciary duty where the government’s conduct involves communications, but does not satisfy the elements of a claim for misrepresentation under state law.

  2. Whether the misrepresentation exception of the Federal Tort Claims Act bars claims for aiding and abetting a breach of fiduciary duty because the government’s conduct aided a third party in committing the tort of misrepresentation.
