Supreme Court of the United States · No. 25-7266

Katie Young v. United States District Court for the Northern District of Mississippi

IFP petition · United States Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit, No. 25-60651 · judgment March 9, 2026

Certiorari denied · June 29, 2026

Questions presented

  1. Whether a judicial officer violates the First Amendment by making statements that disparage a litigant’s Religious Beliefs, demonstrating hostility toward Religion.

  2. Whether dismissal of a complaint as frivolous is proper where the complaint alleges Religion Discrimination, First Amendment Violations, and judicial bias.

  3. Whether due process is violated when a litigant’s case is adjudicated by a judicial officer demonstrating Religious hostility or bias.

  4. Whether a court of appeals may dismiss a pro se litigant’s appeal as frivolous without addressing constitutional claims on the merits based on a determination that the appellant failed to “meaningfully challenge” the district court’s reasoning.
