Question presented

Is the Third Circuit acting in opposition to controlling law (Erickson v. Pardus, Johnson v. City of Shelby) and different from other circuits by implementing a heightened pleading standard? Is the Third Circuit violating a citizen's state rights in dismissing state claims with prejudice for lack of subject matter jurisdiction? Are they acting in contrast to other circuits as it pertains to 28 U.S. Code §163 1? Is the Third Circuit depriving rights by its departures from the rules authorized by Congress by 28 U.S. Code §2072? Does the Third Circuit's rules on finality conflict with 28 U.S. Code § 1291? Is the Third Circuit split from the 9th Circuit as to the time to appeal from alternate (non-final) rulings? Is a matter appealable as of right if a federal question (28 U.S. Code § 1331) has not been adjudicated by the court of first review?
