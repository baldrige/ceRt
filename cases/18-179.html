Questions presented

  1. Whether the Court should resolve the following question for which the state courts are split (including the Florida appellate court of last resort in this case): can law enforcement officers rely on the exigent circumstance exception to the Fourth Amendment warrant requirement to justify the warrantless extraction of blood from a suspected drunk driver when the officers make no attempt to obtain a warrant prior to forcibly taking the blood sample and presented no evidence that a warrant judge was actually unavailable (which, in essence, would create another per se exigency in contravention of the Court’s holding in Missouri v. McNeely, 569 U.S. 141 (2013)).
