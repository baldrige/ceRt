Question presented

Contracts are composed of words. If words in con­ tracts vary in their meanings, the contract is in peril of being void and unenforceable. Each word of a contract comprises a legal element which should be clear and unambiguous. The question posed herein is in reference to the words used in contracts and agreements containing the phrase, “new fact;” and what qualifies as a “new fact.” The question presented is: In contracts, after signing an agreement, is the term, “new fact,” applicable to a fact that is new and previously unknown to one party, or new and previously unknown to both (all) parties?
