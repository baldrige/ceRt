Questions presented

Fannie and Freddie buy and securitize residential mortgages. In 2008, the Federal Housing Finance Authority (FHFA or Agency) put Fannie and Freddie into conservatorship. A federal statute provides that “[n]o property of the [FHFA] shall be subject to levy, attachment, garnishment, foreclosure, or sale without the consent of the Agency.” 12 U.S.C. § 4617(j)(3). Because Fannie and Freddie regularly fail to record their interest in a property, many properties are foreclosed upon in potential violation of this provision. FHFA has therefore frequently filed quiet title actions asserting that Fannie or Freddie’s mortgages were not extinguished by a foreclosure sale. In M&T Bank v. SFR Investments Pool 1, LLC, 963 F.3d 854, 858 (9th Cir. 2020), the Ninth Circuit held that these quiet title actions are subject to a federal statute of limitations for “contract claims” by FHFA, 12 U.S.C. § 4617(b)(12). *
