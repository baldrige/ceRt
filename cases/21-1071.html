Questions presented

  1. Can the government deputize private actors to attack political speech and thereby abridge political speech using law and case law in civil court?

  2. Can a state civil court classify political speech as a crime without witness or discovery and relying on undisputed perjury, and dismiss a defamation lawsuit, whether compelled to do so by law and code of the state or with discretion, with the effect of protecting and participating as instrument in a government attack on political speech, by stripping a private citizen of traditional protections against defamation, in a special circumstance contrived by government in its own interest, to selectively deputize and bolster and reward a non-government actor, toward the purpose of using false and malicious defamation to penalize the private citizen in retaliation for speech petitioning that same government with grievances?
