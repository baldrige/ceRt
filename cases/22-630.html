Question presented

  1. Should this Court extend its holding in Piper Aircraft Co. v. Reyno, 454 U.S. 235 (1981) and hold that where a foreign plaintiff has made a wellsupported claim for punitive damages against an American corporation for American-based punitive conduct which caused harm abroad, the unavailability of a punitive remedy in an alternative forum shall weigh against dismissal of the case for forum non conveniens?

  2. If a district court finds that there is a wellsupported claim for punitive damages arising out of American-based punitive conduct but determines that the case should be dismissed for forum non conveniens, is the court permitted to implement a return-jurisdiction condition on its dismissal allowing for the punitive damages claim to ultimately be tried in the United States?
