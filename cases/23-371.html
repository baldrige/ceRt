Question presented

I. 28 U.S.C. § 1259(3) states that this Court may review “[c]ases in which the Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces granted a petition for review.” Does 28 U.S.C. § 1259(3) give this Court jurisdiction, as part of the case, to consider issues raised to, but not granted review by, the Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces? II. Is Second Lieutenant Johnson’s conviction for sexually assaulting M.P. legally sufficient? 1 1 Second Lieutenant Johnson raised Issue II to the Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces, but it did not grant his petition for review on this issue.
