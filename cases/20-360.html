Question presented

The Prison Litigation Reform Act (“PLRA”) prescribes precisely how to calculate an award of attorneys’ fees in prisoner cases. See 42 U.S.C. § 1997e(d). This Court and several circuit courts have recognized that the PLRA’s fee formula, not the traditional lodestar method, applies and limits a district court’s discretion to award more than what it expressly authorizes. The Ninth Circuit, however, followed its own precedent and held that a district court retains the discretion to enhance a fee award, notwithstanding the PLRA’s limitations. The question presented is whether the PLRA leaves any room for a district court to enhance a fee award in prisoner cases beyond what it statutorily prescribes.
