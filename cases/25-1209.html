Questions presented

  1. Whether the two-year time limit for bringing a civil action for damages against the United States under 26 U.S.C. § 7433(d)(3) is a jurisdictional bar that strips a federal court of its power to hear a case, or a non-jurisdictional claim-processing rule subject to equitable tolling.

  2. Whether the Ninth Circuit’s holding that tax­ code limitations periods are jurisdictional— relying on Libitzky v. United States, 110 F.4th 1166 (9th Cir. 2024)—directly conflicts with the "clear statement" rule established by this Court in Boechler, P.C. v. Commissioner, 596 U.S. 199 (2022).
