Question presented

This Court has long held that a state official does not lose the federal defense of qualified immunity merely because he has acted inconsistently with a state law. See Davis v. Scherer, 468 U.S. 183 (1984). This is so even if the state official “ignores a clear legal command” found in state law. Id. at 194. This case presents the following question: Whether a state official’s qualified immunity defense to a claim for money damages necessarily fails if he cannot first prove that he had authority under state law to perform the challenged act.
