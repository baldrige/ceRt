Question presented

The Federal Tort Claims Act’s law enforcement proviso, 28 U.S.C. § 2680(h), waives the United States’ sovereign immunity for “[a]ny claim” arising out of an enumerated list of intentional common-law torts committed by federal law enforcement officers. The question presented is whether, and to what extent, the discretionary function exception to the Federal Tort Claims Act, 28 U.S.C. § 2680(a), restricts that proviso.
