Supreme Court of the United States · No. 25-7167

In Re Eli Angelino

IFP petition

Certiorari denied · June 15, 2026

Questions presented

  1. Whether a Clerk of Court violates the First and Fourteenth Amendments by "misconstruing" an official judicial record—specifically by maintaining a fraudulent "IFP Pending" status for over 70 days despite a prior fee waiver (Entry 31)—to block the entry of a $100,000,000.00 default judgment?

  2. Whether a Clerk of Court violates the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment by imposing a "clerical embargo" on a pro se litigant—holding curative filings (Entries 87-107) in a perpetual "Received" status—to prevent the case from reaching a Judge for a ruling on the merits?

  3. Whether a Court of Appeals may constructively deny the right of access to the courts by indefinitely withholding a ruling on an In Forma Pauperis (IFP) application while allowing a "default" clock to run against an indigent petitioner who has provided forensic proof of identity?
