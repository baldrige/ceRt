Questions presented

  1. Does a drug-conspiracy defendant - having executed a Rule 11(c)(1)(C) plea agreement in which he waives appeal of sentence, reserves his right to challenge the government’s drug-quantity estimate at sentencing, and reserves "all rights" arising from application of the United States Sentencing Guidelines - preserve his right to appeal an erroneous drug quantity determination?

  2. Does a drug-conspiracy defendant - having executed a Rule 11(c)(1)(C) plea agreement in which he waives appeal of sentence, reserves his right to challenge the government’s drug-quantity estimate at sentencing, and reserves "all rights" arising from application of the United States Sentencing Guidelines - preserve his right to appeal of sentence where the sentencing court makes no finding of the drug quantity reasonably foreseeable by the defendant individually and instead shifts to the defendant the drug quantity attributable to the conspiracy as a whole?
