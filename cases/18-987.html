Question presented

This Court has repeatedly said that a plaintiff seeking class certification under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 23(b)(3) bears the burden of proving that common issues predominate over individualized ones. E.g., Halliburton Co. v. Erica P. John Fund, Inc., 573 U.S. 258, 275 (2014). The question presented is whether the burden at class certification shifts to the defendant when predominance turns on affirmative defenses.
