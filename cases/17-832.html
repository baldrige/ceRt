Question presented

Whether the United States Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit and District Court materially erred and turned a blind eye and deaf ear on what constitutes clear and uncontrovertible proof positive of actual and factual innocence of the Petitioner on Counts 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, and 7 when the District Court repeatedly denied the trial jury the opportunity to see and hear clearly exculpatory evidence thereby giving rise to a series of structural Constitutional violations requiring this Court to assume authority over the case and ORDER reversal of the wrongful convictions complained of by the Petitioner, with prejudice, and substantiating Petitioner's ongoing claims of systemic prejudice and bias on the part of the trial Court, rendering the underlying convictions null and void, ab initio. Whether the District Court materially erred when it disregarded the ruling of the Supreme Court in Stern v. US., 543 U.S. 1097, 125 S.Ct. 988, 160 L.Ed. 996 (2005) and resentenced the Petitioner to 124 months confinement making the resentencing illegal and substantiating Petitioner's ongoing claims of systemic personal animus as well as prejudice and bias on the part of the trial Court, rendering the underlying convictions null and void, ab initio.
