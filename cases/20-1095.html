Question presented

Whether a criminal defendant’s purported waiver of the right to appeal in a plea agreement is knowing, intelligent, and voluntary—as required by the Due Process Clauses of the Fifth and Fourteenth Amendments—when the trial court incorrectly informs the defendant, during the colloquy in which the court accepts the defendant’s guilty plea, that the defendant has reserved the right to appeal.
