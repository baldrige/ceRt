Questions presented

  1. Whether a trial court must grant a motion for acquittal when, in viewing the evidence in the light most favorable to the prosecution, the evidence provides an equal likelihood of guilt and innocence.

  2. Whether Hobbs Act robbery constitutes a “crime of violence” under 18 U.S.C. § 924(c)(3)(A) when it can be committed through mere fear of future injury to property.
