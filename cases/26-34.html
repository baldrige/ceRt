Question presented

Under Missouri law, convicted sex offenders cannot participate in Halloween: They must avoid all Halloween-related contact with children, remain at their residence with all outside lighting off during the evening hours, and post a notice stating “No candy or treats at this residence.” Thomas Sanderson, a convicted sex offender, challenged the sign-posting requirement as unconstitutional compelled speech. The Eighth Circuit agreed and struck down Missouri’s law—contending that “the sign mandate compels speech” and fails strict scrutiny. The question presented is: Does Missouri’s Halloween sign-posting requirement, which requires factually accurate disclosures, unconstitutionally compel the speech of convicted sex offenders?
