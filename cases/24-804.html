Question presented

The Commissioner of Internal Revenue, a federal agency, applied federal income tax to the on-reservation income of an Indian despite a constitutional provision that Indians Not [be] Taxed and in the absence of an Act of Congress clearly authorizing the tax. The question presented is: Did the Court of Appeals err in upholding federal income tax on an individual Indian’s self-employment income earned on his Tribe’s reservation without Treaty, constitutional, or congressional expressly stated authorization for that taxation?
