Question(s) presentedCan the People rely upon the plain language of the law to exercise their Constitutional Rights? Or must the People succumb to egregious precedents of this Court in error? The questions presented are: 1. Do individual judges have the Constitutional authority and judicial power to follow the plain lan­ guage of the law, or is vertical stare decisis absolute regardless of egregious precedents? 2. Did this Court exercise its will in United States v. Jones, 131 U. S. 1 (1889) instead of the plain lan­ guage of the law — the clear intent of Congress? 3. Do Dreiling’s original claims satisfy the plain language of the Tucker Act, and if so, is he entitled to rely upon that plain language?
