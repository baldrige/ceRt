This case concerns the judicially created damages remedy first recognized in Bivens v. Six Unknown Named Agents of Federal Bureau of Narcotics, 403 U.S. 388 (1971). In Carlson v. The Ninth Circuit held the suit did not present a “new” context from Carlson—notwithstanding an alternative remedial scheme this Court did not consider in Carlson, and despite significant factual differences such as the immediacy and severity of the harm alleged. The question presented is: Whether the Ninth Circuit here erred in recognizing a Bivens cause of action. (i)
